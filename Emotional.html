Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and Its Principles
  • Cognition means Thought, Way of Thinking, Believe, Perception, or Interpretation.
  • Three principles of CBT:
    • First Principle: Cognition, Thought, Way of Thinking, Believe, Perception, Interpretation are the major cause of our bad/good emotions, feelings or moods.
      However, sometimes our feeling/moods/emotions creates certain kinds thoughts. For example, when you are physically ill, you may get a different kinds of emotions, and that generates a very different kinds of thoughts you generally experience when you are not feeling well. When you go to temple, you may get different types of emotions, and thus you have completely different thoughs. In Contrast, circumstance of our life are not the main cause of our good/bad feelings, on which we generally belief.
    • Second Principle: When you are upset, your thoughts are dominated by pervasive negativity/ negative thoughts. (I will explain later what do we means by negative thoughts?)
    • Third Principle: The negative thoughts that upset you are nearly always distorted and twisted. (There exits about 12 thoughts distortion, that I will discribe later in this note.)
  • The core of message of CBT is that when you change the way you think, You can change the way you feel.
  • Various quotes also support the core message, such as,
    • Epictetus said in about 2000 years ago: People are disturbed “not by things, but by the views we take of them.”
    • Shakespeare: “For there is nothing either good or bad, but thinking makes it so.”
    • Lori Deschene on Buddha’s statement: Thoughts fuel emotions. If you don’t like what you’re feeling, step back and examine what you’re thinking.”
