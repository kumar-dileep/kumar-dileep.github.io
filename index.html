About me
I am a PhD research scholar at IIT Kanpur in Control and Automation in the Department of Electrical Engineering. Prior to Joining IIT Kanpur, I worked as an Assistant Professor at Department of Electrical Engineering, NIET, NIMS Jaipur. I did M.Tech in Control Systems Engineering from NIT Patna and B.Tech from SMVDU, Katra, J&K.
My Research
My PhD thesis investigates EVs with in-wheel motors (IWMs) from two perspectives: energy usage and tire usage.

Towards energy usage, the thesis examines EVs with IWMs from two perspectives: energy usage and tire usage. Towards energy usage, It calculates tank-to-wheel (TTW) energy efficiency of Battery- powered EVs (BEVs). These BEVs are bikes, microcars, mid-size cars, full-size cars, and buses. They lie in wide power intervals and widely use single motor and single/multiple IWMs.

Towards tire usage, It primarily considers four-wheel independent steering four-wheel independent drive (4WIS4WID) EVs, as they have the potential to achieve a condition known as optimal tire usage.

My M.Tech thesis studies the modeling of battery, fuel cell, and photovoltaic array available with MATLAB/Simulink. it performs simulation of integrated photovoltaic cell, fuel cell, and battery-based distributed generation system. It also develops a smart backup system for photovoltaic array in MATLAB/Simulink.

My B.Tech projects are primarily focused on demonstrating full-duplex communications with multiple (three) 8051 kits and computers. In another project, It demonstrates the same with Sparton 3e kits and computers using VHDL programming.
